The Unit of One Manifesto

Dispatch 002 | May 31, 2026

Don't you dare call me "Single." That word is a fucking lazy, corporate hand-wave used by healthcare ghouls and insurance drones to ignore the reality of human survival. "Single" implies you’re just a person between dates. But a Unit of One is someone between the floor and the ceiling with no one coming to help them get up. We are the individuals the system is designed to glitch on because we don't have anyone from a caring family or a spouse to hold the ladder. Let alone drive us home from an ACDF surgery. We are our own advocates, our own nurses, and our own administrative assistants fighting a 200% tax on every goddamn task just to exist.

The status of a Unit of One often gets underlooked because life circumstances can’t be photographed and plastered on our foreheads. And, it's a Logistics of One forged in the fires of systemic erasure and family abandonment. It’s born the second you realize your biological safety net was shredded by religious indoctrination and bigotry, leaving you to face permanent paralysis or medical neglect in total isolation. This is the raw, exhausting crucible of navigating a world built for couples and case numbers while you serve as the only foundation for your own survival. We aren't just "living alone.” Not at fucking all! We’re surviving a medical industrial complex that refuses to see the human behind the billing code when there is no "emergency contact" to call.

This is a total binary re-classification—a middle finger to the modern healthcare landscape that assumes every patient has at least a "chosen family" waiting in the wings. We’re aggressively restructuring the way needs are assessed because our dignity is not up for negotiation. This movement is about shattering the assumption that community always exists, especially for rural queer individuals who are often "the only ones" in their orbit. We demand to be recognized as an unbreakable Unit of One, not as a "patient deficit" to be dismissed. We are the architects of our own survival, and by God, we will be seen.
