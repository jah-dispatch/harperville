Happy 70th Birthday, Mom!

Dispatch 007 | July 7, 2026

The heavens did bestow a wink most cosmic upon the day just passed. Mark you, 'twas the seventieth anniversary of my lady mother’s birth, a milestone most worthy of high honor though she hath long since departed this mortal coil.

In her memory, I did indulge in the potent herb and partook of a feast of golden arches, e'er treading a measure with the fair Ivy Willow—a stout companion of ten pounds and more. Upon the wings of helium did I dispatch a missive to the clouds.

Though the wise may chide the folly of such air-borne parchment, 'tis a rite I hold sacred. Yet, by some whimsical fate, the spheres did tangle with the very wires that carry our worldly discourse.

Entering mine abode, I did break bread and cake, then cleansed my form in waters cold before seeking once more the solace of the indica flower. Alas, the mending of my flesh from the surgeon’s blade goeth but haltingly—some dawns are bright with hope, yet others find my neck encased as if in iron, my spine a-quiver with such discord that one might fear the physician’s art had failed.

Still, I render thanks for the love that floweth betwixt my mother’s spirit and mine own, and for Ivy, a champion of true heart.

Be advised, a new chronicle entitled "Hemingway Falls" shall emerge at the season of Thanksgiving—a triumphant return for this humble scribe. Rejoice that the sluggish wheels of the great printing houses do not delay us, for in independence lieth our beauty. I bid thee all a day of wonder!

Let it be known that the tenth of July shall henceforth be marked as the day I did receive my new appellation by decree of the court.
