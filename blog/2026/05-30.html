My Cat Saves My Life Every Day

Dispatch 001 | May 30, 2026

Navigating the reality of structural failures and systemic inertia as a Unit of One can easily turn the daily grind into a battle of attrition. When the horizon is dictated by upcoming surgical dates and a compromised nervous system, it’s incredibly easy to let the walls close in. But there’s a persistent, four-legged counterweight in the room—a tortoiseshell anchor named Ivy Willow who remains entirely unimpressed by medical pipelines, state bureaucracies, or impending hard stops.

Every morning before dawn breaks, she demands radical execution. Bending down to retrieve airborne scrunchy toys might look like basic pet ownership to a casual observer, but when every physical pivot is a calculated negotiation with a stubborn spine, it’s a masterclass in maintaining volition. She forces presence. She doesn't allow room for passive detachment; she insists that if there is mechanical capability left in the frame, it will be used to engage.

This was a test post. My Cat? Does she really save my life every day? Absolutely. As a person on the schizophrenia spectrum, yes 100%, Ivy Willow Harper is one of my angels.
